Type of Visas

Visit to UK

Certain nationalities do not require a visa to visit the UK. However, if you have previously been refused a visa or entry to the UK, this exemption does not apply. Please contact us to see if you require a visa.

In most cases the visit category allows a stay of up to six months. For a stay longer than six months, please refer to our Study in the UK, Work in the UK & Live in the UK pages for further guidance.

What is the purpose of your visit to the UK? You will need to select a sub-category of visit based on whether you are a tourist, visiting family or attending business meetings etc. Please see below to select which sub-category is most suitable for you.

  • General Visitor
  • Family Visitor
  • Child Visitor
  • Business Visitor
  • Student Visitor
  • Sports Visitor
  • Entertainer Visitor
  • Visit for Marriage / Civil Partnership
  • Visit for Private Medical Treatment
  • Visitor in Transit
  • Prospective Student

Once you have chosen your category (and confirmed with us that it is correct), you may retain our services by paying our consultation fee

Study in UK

If you are a visa national wishing to study in the UK for less than six months and you do not intend to engage in any employment, paid or unpaid, or subsequently extend your stay in the UK, you may qualify for a Student Visitor visa.

If you are a non-visa national then you will not require a visa. However, you will need to show evidence of your acceptance on a course of study and proof that you have sufficient funds to support yourself for the duration of your stay upon arriving in the UK.

Please contact us for further information.

Work in UK

There are many different visas available for those wishing to work in the UK. The different categories are:

Points Based System Categories (also known as PBS)

  • Tier 1 is for high-value migrants - no job offer required
  • Tier 2 is for skilled workers - requires a job offer and a Certificate of Sponsorship (also known as a COS) from an organization that is a licensed sponsor.
  • Tier 5 is for temporary workers - requires a job offer and a Certificate of Sponsorship (also known as a COS) from an organization that is a licensed sponsor.

You will need to fulfill different requirements for each category to pass a points based assessment. The points based system assesses your eligibility to work in the UK based on your answers to a series of questions specific to your circumstances.
